Mother Teresa

The Saint of the Gutters

Agnes Gonxha Bojaxhiu, more commonly known as Mother Teresa, was a Roman Catholic nun who devoted her life to serving the poor and destitute around the world. Born on August 26, 1910 in Skopje, Macedonia, Mother Teresa left home at the age of 18 to join the Sisters of Loreto in Ireland. She then moved to India in 1929, where she taught at a school in Darjeeling before eventually founding the Missionaries of Charity in 1950.

Serving India

Mother Teresa dedicated her life to serving the poor and sick in India, and her work quickly gained recognition around the world. Her organization, the Missionaries of Charity, expanded rapidly and eventually established missions in countries around the globe. Mother Teresa's selfless service to others earned her numerous awards and accolades, including the Nobel Peace Prize in 1979.

Awards and Recognition

  • Nobel Peace Prize (1979)
  • Bharat Ratna (1980)
  • Presidential Medal of Freedom (1985)
  • Order of Merit (1986)
  • Congressional Gold Medal (1997)

Legacy

Mother Teresa's legacy lives on today through the work of the Missionaries of Charity, which continues to serve the poor and sick around the world. She is remembered as a tireless advocate for those in need, and her commitment to serving others continues to inspire people of all faiths and backgrounds.

"Spread love everywhere you go. Let no one ever come to you without leaving happier." - Mother Teresa
